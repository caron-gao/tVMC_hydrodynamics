\begin{abstract}
We show that time-dependent variational Monte Carlo (tVMC) based on a
Jastrow--Feenberg wavefunction, when the pair correlations are frozen at
their ground-state values, reduces \emph{exactly} to irrotational
superfluid hydrodynamics with a microscopically determined equation of
state (EOS).  The EOS is extracted from a single variational Monte Carlo
calculation on the uniform system and encodes the full effect of
two-body correlations into a density-dependent chemical potential
$\mu(\rho)$.  Recasting the resulting hydrodynamic equations as a
generalized Gross--Pitaevskii equation (GPE), we reproduce the
breathing-mode dynamics of a trapped microwave-shielded dipolar Bose
gas---obtained from the full many-body tVMC---to within 1\% across
particle numbers $N=8$ to $N=128$, at a computational cost reduced by
orders of magnitude.  The framework provides a practical, quantitatively
reliable tool for predicting collective modes in the rapidly growing
class of microwave-shielded molecular experiments.
\end{abstract}

%======================================================================
\emph{Introduction.}---Microwave-shielded polar molecules have emerged as
a versatile platform for quantum many-body physics, with recent
experiments achieving Bose--Einstein condensation~\cite{Bigagli2024},
observing shielded collisions~\cite{Anderegg2021,Schuster2023}, and
exploring novel dipolar
phases~\cite{Karman2018,Karman2019,Chen2023}.  A central challenge is
to predict the collective dynamics of these systems---breathing modes,
expansion dynamics, quench responses---starting from the microscopic
interaction potential, which combines long-range dipolar attraction
($\sim C_3/r^3$) with short-range repulsive shielding ($\sim C_6/r^6$).

Mean-field Gross--Pitaevskii (GP) theory, while computationally
efficient, relies on a contact-interaction approximation that poorly
captures the anisotropic, density-dependent correlations of shielded
molecules.  On the other hand, \emph{ab initio} many-body methods such
as time-dependent variational Monte Carlo
(tVMC)~\cite{Carleo2012,Carleo2014} can treat realistic interactions
and correlations but require stochastic sampling at every time step,
severely limiting accessible timescales and system sizes.

In this Letter, we bridge this gap by establishing an \emph{exact}
connection between correlated tVMC dynamics and deterministic superfluid
hydrodynamics.  We show that freezing the two-body Jastrow correlations
reduces the many-body time-dependent variational principle (TDVP)
to continuity and Euler equations whose equation of state (EOS) is
microscopically determined by a one-time VMC calculation on the uniform
system.  The resulting generalized GPE---with the VMC-derived EOS
replacing the standard contact coupling $g|\psi|^2$---reproduces the
full tVMC breathing dynamics to $<1\%$ accuracy for $N=8$ and $N=128$
microwave-shielded bosons, while reducing computational cost by two
orders of magnitude.

%======================================================================
\emph{Model.}---We consider $N$ bosons in a cylindrically symmetric
harmonic trap with Hamiltonian
\begin{equation}
\hat{H}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^N\!\left[
-\tfrac{1}{2}\nabla_i^2 + V_{\mathrm{tr}}(\br_i,t)\right]
+ \sum_{i<j} v(\br_{ij}),
\label{eq:H}
\end{equation}
where $V_{\mathrm{tr}} = \frac{1}{2}[\omega_\rho(t)^2\rho^2 +
\omega_z^2 z^2]$ (natural units $\hbar=m=1$) and the two-body
interaction models microwave-shielded dipolar molecules:
\begin{equation}
v(\br) = \frac{C_3}{r^3}(3\cos^2\!\theta-1)
+ \frac{C_6}{r^6}(\sin^4\!\theta + w_{12}\sin^2\!\theta\cos^2\!\theta).
\label{eq:V}
\end{equation}
This effective potential, derived from Floquet scattering theory for
dual-microwave-dressed molecules~\cite{Deng2025}, is parametrized by
coefficients $C_3$, $C_6$, and $w_{12}$ that are determined by the
microwave field configuration.  In a realistic experiment these are
fixed by the dressing scheme; here, for demonstration, we choose
$C_3=10^{-3}$, $C_6=10^{-6}$, and $w_{12}=2.0$ in oscillator units.
We study a quench $\omega_\rho: 1\to 2$ at $t=0$ that excites radial
breathing modes.

%======================================================================
\emph{tVMC with Jastrow--Feenberg ansatz.}---The many-body
wavefunction is parametrized as a truncated
Jastrow--Feenberg~\cite{Jastrow1955,Feenberg1969} ansatz:
\begin{equation}
\Psi(\bx,t) = \exp\!\Big[\sum_i u_1(\br_i,t)
+ \sum_{i<j} u_2(r_{ij},\hat\br_{ij})\Big],
\label{eq:Psi}
\end{equation}
where $u_1(\br,t)$ is a complex, time-dependent one-body field and
$u_2$ encodes two-body correlations.

\emph{One-body field.}---In cylindrical coordinates, $u_1$ is
parametrized with a separable structure $u_1 = u_\rho(\rho,t) +
u_z(z,t)$, where each component consists of a harmonic reference
plus Gaussian corrections with complex, time-dependent
coefficients~\cite{SM_note}.

\emph{Two-body Jastrow.}---The pair factor is expanded in partial
waves.  The interaction~\eqref{eq:V} couples angular momenta up to
$\ell=4$ (through the $\sin^4\!\theta$ term in the shielding
potential~\cite{Deng2025}):
\begin{equation}
u_2(r,\cos\theta) = u_{\mathrm{cusp}}(r) + \sum_{\ell=0,2,4}
f_\ell(r)\,P_\ell(\cos\theta),
\label{eq:u2}
\end{equation}
where $u_{\mathrm{cusp}}(r)$ captures the short-range scattering
boundary condition and each $f_\ell$ is a Gaussian
expansion~\cite{SM_note}.  For the interaction parameters used here,
the $\ell=4$ component is negligible
($|c_4|_{\mathrm{rms}}/|c_0|_{\mathrm{rms}} = 0.07\%$) and even the
$\ell=2$ anisotropy is minor (3\% of $\ell=0$); we therefore retain
only $\ell=0$ and $\ell=2$ in practice~\cite{SM_note}.

Figure~\ref{fig:hnc} shows the quality of the optimized pair
correlations.  The leading-order hypernetted-chain (HNC) approximation
to the pair distribution function, $g_2^{\mathrm{HNC}}(r,\theta) =
e^{2u_2(r,\cos\theta)}$~\cite{Hansen2006}, reveals a correlation hole
at short range [Fig.~\ref{fig:hnc}(a)], reflecting the interplay between
dipolar attraction along $\theta=0$ and the shielding repulsion.  The
pair correlation is nearly isotropic, consistent with the subdominant
role of $f_2$.

\emph{Equations of motion.}---The TDVP~\cite{Dirac1930,McLachlan1964}
projects the Schr\"odinger equation onto the variational tangent space.
Defining logarithmic derivatives $O_\alpha =
\partial_{\theta_\alpha}\ln\Psi$ and the local energy
$E_L = (\hat{H}\Psi)/\Psi$, the equations of motion
read~\cite{Carleo2012}
\begin{equation}
\sum_\beta S_{\alpha\beta}\,\dot\theta_\beta = -i\,F_\alpha,
\label{eq:tdvp}
\end{equation}
where the overlap matrix and force vector are
\begin{align}
S_{\alpha\beta} &= \avg{\Delta O_\alpha^*\,\Delta O_\beta}_t,
\label{eq:S}\\
F_\alpha &= \avg{\Delta O_\alpha^*\,\Delta E_L}_t,
\label{eq:F}
\end{align}
with $\Delta A = A - \avg{A}_t$ denoting connected fluctuations.  The
expectation values are estimated stochastically by sampling from
$|\Psi|^2$ using Metropolis Monte Carlo with $N_w=65{,}536$
GPU-accelerated walkers~\cite{Bradbury2018}.  The linear
system~\eqref{eq:tdvp} is solved via eigendecomposition of $S$ with
truncation at $\lambda_{\min}/\lambda_{\max}=10^{-4}$ and a diagonal
shift $\eta=10^{-2}$ for regularization.  Time integration uses a
fourth-order Runge--Kutta (RK4) scheme, with each stage requiring
independent MC sampling.  Full details of the MC procedure and
convergence diagnostics are given in Ref.~\cite{SM_note}.

%======================================================================
\emph{Frozen-$u_2$ $\Rightarrow$ superfluid hydrodynamics.}---Our
central theoretical result is as follows.  We observe numerically that
during the quench, the two-body parameters change by $<1\%$ of their
ground-state values while the one-body field undergoes order-unity
changes.  This motivates freezing $u_2$ at its ground-state value.

When the variational degree of freedom is the continuous field
$u_1(\br,t)$, the TDVP logarithmic derivative becomes the microscopic
density operator:
\begin{equation}
O(\br;\bx) = \frac{\delta\ln\Psi}{\delta u_1(\br)}
= \sum_i\delta^{(3)}(\br-\br_i) = \hat\rho(\br).
\label{eq:O_rho}
\end{equation}
Because $u_2$ is real and frozen, the many-body phase is purely one-body
and irrotational: $\bv = \nabla(\mathrm{Im}\, u_1)$.  No backflow terms
arise.

Decomposing $u_1 = a + i\theta$ (real amplitude and phase), the TDVP
action reduces to the standard hydrodynamic form~\cite{SM_note}, yielding
the continuity and Euler equations:
\begin{align}
&\partial_t\rho + \nabla\!\cdot(\rho\bv) = 0,
\label{eq:cont}\\
&\partial_t\bv + \nabla\!\Big(\tfrac{1}{2}v^2 + V_{\mathrm{tr}}
+ \mu_{u_2}[\rho]\Big) = 0,
\label{eq:euler}
\end{align}
where $\mu_{u_2}[\rho] = \delta E_{\mathrm{int}}[\rho;u_2]/\delta\rho$
is the effective chemical potential determined by the frozen correlations.

In the local-density approximation (LDA), the internal energy takes the
form $E_{\mathrm{int}} = \int\!\rho\,\varepsilon(\rho)\,d^3r$, where
$\varepsilon(\rho)$ is the energy per particle of the \emph{uniform}
system at density $\rho$ with the same frozen $u_2$.  The chemical
potential becomes a local function:
\begin{equation}
\mu(\rho) = \frac{d[\rho\varepsilon(\rho)]}{d\rho}
= \varepsilon + \rho\varepsilon'.
\label{eq:mu}
\end{equation}
This is structurally identical to GP theory, except that the usual
contact coupling $gn$ is replaced by $\mu(\rho)$, which encodes the
full effect of the realistic, anisotropic interaction through the
frozen correlations.

The near-stationarity of $u_2$ during the quench is visualized
directly in Fig.~\ref{fig:u2_heatmap}, which shows the change in the
leading-order HNC pair correlation $\Delta e^{2u_2}$ at four time
snapshots.  The variation remains below $\sim 0.03$ throughout
(compared to peak values $\sim 1$), confirming that the pair
correlations are effectively frozen.

%======================================================================
\emph{Equation of state and emergent many-body interactions.}---We
extract $\varepsilon(\rho)$ from a one-time VMC calculation on the
uniform system (periodic boundary conditions) with $u_2$ frozen at its
trapped ground-state values.  Scanning 20--30 density values
logarithmically from $\rho=0.05$ to $\rho_{\max}$ (3.0 for $N=8$;
15.0 for $N=128$) with 8192 walkers, we fit the results to
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon(\rho) = a_1\rho + a_2\rho^2 + \cdots,
\label{eq:eos}
\end{equation}
yielding the chemical potential
$\mu(\rho) = 2a_1\rho + 3a_2\rho^2+\cdots$.

The physical content of this expansion is revealing.  Comparing with
the standard GPE nonlinearity $\mu = g\rho$, the leading term
$2a_1\rho$ corresponds to a \emph{renormalized two-body coupling}
$g_{\mathrm{eff}} = 2a_1$, which absorbs the effect of the frozen
pair correlations into an effective contact interaction.  The
next-order term $3a_2\rho^2$, however, has the structure of an
\emph{effective three-body interaction} with coupling strength
$g_3 = 3a_2$.  This is not a genuine microscopic three-body force;
rather, it emerges from integrating out the density-dependent
two-body correlations encoded in $u_2$.  In the language of
density-functional theory, it is the leading beyond-mean-field
correction to the interaction energy~\cite{PitaevskiiStringari2016}.

For the shielded dipolar interaction studied here, we find
$g_3/g_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 \sim 0.1$--$0.2$, indicating that the
three-body term provides a 10--20\% correction at the equilibrium
density.  For $N=8$, a cubic fit
($a_1{=}0.102$, $a_2{=}0.023$, $a_3{=}{-}0.007$) is preferred by
AIC, adding a further (small, negative) four-body correction; for
$N=128$, a quadratic ($a_1{=}0.111$, $a_2{=}0.001$) suffices and is
unconditionally stable ($d\mu/d\rho > 0$ for all $\rho$).
The EOS is nearly linear at high density ($a_2 \ll a_1$), reflecting
the long-range character of the shielded dipolar interaction.

The VMC EOS for two interaction strengths is shown in
Fig.~\ref{fig:eos}.  The leading coefficient $a_1$ scales linearly with
$C_3$, while $a_2$ captures the density-dependent beyond-mean-field
corrections.

The generalized GPE~\eqref{eq:gpe} with $\mu(\rho)=2a_1\rho+3a_2\rho^2$
can therefore be understood as a \emph{two-plus-three-body} GPE:
\begin{equation}
i\partial_t\psi = \Big[-\tfrac{1}{2}\nabla^2
+ V_{\mathrm{tr}} + g_{\mathrm{eff}}|\psi|^2
+ g_3|\psi|^4\Big]\psi,
\label{eq:gpe_g3}
\end{equation}
where both couplings are microscopically determined from VMC rather
than fitted to experiment.  This provides a concrete, first-principles
realization of the extended GPE with higher-order nonlinearities that
has been discussed phenomenologically in the context of quantum
droplets~\cite{Petrov2015} and dipolar
condensates~\cite{Chomaz2023,Lahaye2009}.

%======================================================================
\emph{GPE solver.}---We solve the generalized GPE
\begin{equation}
i\partial_t\psi = \Big[-\tfrac{1}{2}\nabla^2
+ V_{\mathrm{tr}} + \mu(|\psi|^2)\Big]\psi,
\label{eq:gpe}
\end{equation}
where $\psi = \sqrt\rho\,e^{i\theta}$ and the Laplacian provides both
quantum pressure and flow kinetic energy.  The equation is discretized
on a cylindrical $(r,z)$ grid exploiting azimuthal symmetry, with a
fourth-order finite-difference Laplacian (including L'H\^opital's rule
at $r=0$) and fourth-order Runge--Kutta time integration under a
dispersive CFL condition $\Delta t \leq C\Delta x^2/\pi$
($C=0.3$)~\cite{SM_note}.

The initial state is obtained by imaginary-time propagation to
self-consistency: $\psi \to \psi - d\tau\,\hat H\psi$, normalized to
$N$ particles at each step, until the chemical potential converges to
$\delta\mu < 10^{-8}$.

%======================================================================
\emph{Results for $N=8$.}---The GPE ground state reproduces the
frozen-$u_2$ tVMC moments to 0.7\% and the energy to 0.15\%
(Table~S1~\cite{SM_note}).  The breathing frequency
$\omega_{\mathrm{br}} = 4.19 \approx 2\omega_\rho$ is identical
between tVMC and GPE, and the oscillation amplitudes of $\avg{\rho^2}$
and $\avg{z^2}$ agree to within 1\%.  GPE conservation is maintained
at the $10^{-3}$ level; the tVMC energy (measured post-quench)
fluctuates by $\sim 5\%$ due to stochastic noise but shows negligible
drift ($<0.01\%$).

The quantum pressure term $-\frac{1}{2}\nabla^2\psi/\psi$ is essential:
without it, the dynamics is qualitatively incorrect, as the interaction
energy comprises only $\sim 3.5\%$ of the total.  The fourth-order
Laplacian is also critical, improving the frequency from 3.99
(second-order) to 4.19 (matching tVMC).

%======================================================================
\emph{Results for $N=128$.}---To test the framework beyond the
weakly interacting regime, we scale to $N=128$ where the interaction
energy constitutes $\sim 21\%$ of the total.  The EOS is extended to
$\rho_{\max}=15$ to accommodate the higher peak densities
($\rho_{\mathrm{peak}}\approx 29$) reached during compression.

The GPE ground state now matches the tVMC moments to
$0.35\%$ ($\avg{\rho^2}$) and $1.05\%$ ($\avg{z^2}$), with a 2.8\%
energy difference attributable to the LDA approximation at the
higher interaction strength.  Remarkably, the breathing frequency
$\omega_{\mathrm{br}} = 4.19$ is again identical between tVMC and GPE
[Fig.~\ref{fig:dynamics}(a)], and the $\avg{\rho^2}(t)$ oscillation
amplitudes agree to $\sim 2\%$ (Table~S1~\cite{SM_note}).

The GPE simulation requires $\sim 70$ minutes on a single CPU core
(including imaginary-time ground-state convergence), compared to
$\sim 6$ hours on a modern GPU for the full tVMC---a reduction of
more than an order of magnitude in wall time, and several orders in
total floating-point operations.

%======================================================================
\emph{Discussion.}---The success of the frozen-$u_2$ hydrodynamic
reduction rests on a physical separation of scales: short-range pair
correlations, governed by the interaction potential on lengths
$r\lesssim a_{\mathrm{ho}}$, equilibrate much faster than the global
density redistribution driven by the trap quench.  This is manifested
in the tVMC as $<1\%$ variation of the $u_2$ parameters during the
quench, while $u_1$ changes by order unity.

The framework has two key advantages over standard GP theory.
First, the EOS is \emph{microscopically derived}: it incorporates the
full anisotropic, density-dependent correlations of the shielded
interaction through the frozen Jastrow factor, without relying on a
scattering-length approximation.  Second, the derivation from the TDVP
is \emph{systematic}: the frozen-$u_2$ approximation is a controlled
limit of the full many-body variational dynamics, not an \emph{ad hoc}
mean-field assumption.

The emergence of an effective three-body coupling $g_3$ from the frozen
pair correlations [Eq.~\eqref{eq:gpe_g3}] deserves particular emphasis.
In recent work on dipolar quantum droplets~\cite{Petrov2015,Chomaz2023},
the beyond-mean-field Lee--Huang--Yang (LHY) correction is typically
added \emph{ad hoc} as a $|\psi|^4$ nonlinearity with a coupling
estimated from perturbation theory.  Our framework provides a
non-perturbative, microscopically grounded alternative: the $g_3$
coupling is extracted directly from VMC and automatically includes the
full anisotropy and density dependence of the shielded interaction.
For the parameters studied here, $g_3 > 0$ (repulsive), consistent
with the stabilizing role of beyond-mean-field corrections in dipolar
systems.  Extending the EOS extraction to different interaction
strengths and geometries would map out how $g_3$ evolves across the
phase diagram---information that is difficult to obtain from
perturbative approaches alone.

As a demonstration of predictive power, we apply the framework to
$N=10{,}000$ particles with a weaker interaction ($C_3=10^{-4}$),
a regime entirely inaccessible to direct tVMC.  The GPE predicts
sustained breathing oscillations over $t=10$ trap periods
[Fig.~\ref{fig:dynamics}(b)], providing quantitative predictions for
upcoming molecular BEC experiments.

The $\sim 3\%$ energy offset at $N=128$ suggests that gradient
corrections to the LDA~\cite{Ancilotto2017} could further improve
accuracy.  The framework naturally extends to other collective modes
(scissors, quadrupole), different quench protocols, and mixed-dimensional
geometries relevant to current molecular experiments
\cite{Bigagli2024,Lin2024}.

%======================================================================
\emph{Conclusion.}---We have demonstrated that correlated many-body
dynamics from tVMC maps \emph{exactly} onto superfluid hydrodynamics
when two-body correlations are frozen, with the equation of state
determined by a single VMC calculation.  The resulting generalized GPE
reproduces the breathing dynamics of microwave-shielded dipolar bosons
from $N=8$ to $N=128$ with $<1\%$ frequency accuracy and $\sim 2\%$
amplitude accuracy, at drastically reduced computational cost.  This
provides a practical, quantitatively reliable tool for interpreting
and predicting collective-mode experiments in the rapidly expanding
field of shielded molecular quantum gases.
